Let's start from learning letters!


    Types of letters

There are three letters type in Japanese: Hiragana, Katakana and Kanji. Hiragana and Katakana are phonetic symbols, each representing one syllable. Each have 46 letters in total, and these two have same sound but diffrent shape. Hiragana are used for original Japanese such as つくえ(TSUKUE, table), and Katakana used for words from outside of Japan, such as トマト(TOMATO, tomato) Because these two have same sound, you can replace Hiragana and Katakana. For example if you want to write cat in Japanese, you can write in two ways: ねこ and ネコ(NEKO: both pronounce the same, it is just a shape that is diffrent.) Once you memorize Hiragana which is super basic, you can survive in Japan, so don't worry of memorizing every letters!!


    Syllable of Hiragana and Katakana

The vowels are あ(a),い(i),う(u)え(e),お(o), and all other syllable are combined with consonants to form new sounds. For example second row after vowels are vowels + k, so か(ka),き(ki),く(ku)け,(ke)こ(ko). Like this, you combine vowel with consonants such as s, t, n, etc to make saounds.
